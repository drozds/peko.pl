Peko peko is a Japanese expression meaning ‘I’m hungry’. The answer to it is Japanese home cooking – delicious and full of warmth. It would be my pleasure to introduce it to you. Japanese cuisine does not end on the ever-so-popular sushi and ramen. In my restaurant I would like to show you another side of my country’s food. The dishes I serve have been extremely popular in Japan for years and I am sure you will enjoy them as well. I hope the time spent in my restaurant will be an enjoyable and delicious experience!

Karē raisu is a Japanese version of curry, slightly different from popular Indian and Thai dishes. Although it was introduced to Japan via British cuisine, it has been adapted to Japanese cooking, became thicker and gained new ingredients. Japanese curry usually contains meat of some kind, carrots and potatoes.

Donburi is a dish consisting of a bowl of rice with various toppings, very rich and simply delicious. Donburi can be made from almost any ingredients and many traditional Japanese dishes have a donburi version.
